Problem Description

Goals and Deliverables

The goal of this project is to implement three different forms of shading. These are cel shading, watercolor shading and sketch shading. With each different shader, we will render images that show off the different shaders. We aim to answer the question “How can we implement cel shading, watercolor shading and sketch shading for non-photo realistic rendering”. We will measure the quality of our system by measuring the rendering time of each image and rendering a minimum 3 different images per shader.

Cel shading, short for celluloid shading, is a technique that gives 3d models the appearance of a 2d drawing. Instead of rendering with gradients and complex lighting, cel shading uses a simplified model of lighting with just a few flat areas of color. This could be one color for the lit area, one color for the shadow and one color for the highlight. Below are some examples of cel shading.

Watercolor shading is a non photo-realistic rendering technique that aims at mimicking the soft, fluid look of traditional watercolor paintings. This involves gentle color blending, textured brush strokes, and semi-transparent layers. It can also appear uneven or washed out in places to make it look more handmade. This can be achieved through texture overlays (like grain or color bleeding), noise-based transparency of color, and edge darkening that simulates the pooling of paint. Below are examples of watercolor shading.



Sketch shading is a non photo-realistic rendering technique that aims at mimicking the look of hand drawn pencil/pen sketches. This involves hatching, cross-hatching, and rough strokes to create depth and texture, achieved by using procedural line textures, stroke-based shaders, or techniques that detect edges and apply sketch-like marks.
